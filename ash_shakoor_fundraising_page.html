13 Years of Feeding the Hungry in Cape Town

Since 2012, the Ash Shakoor Benevolent Trust has shown up — year after year, season after season — to cook and distribute food to the destitute and most vulnerable communities across the Western Cape.

We are not a large organisation. We are ordinary people who believe that no one should go hungry on the day of Eid. Every year, our volunteers rise before dawn, fire up the pots, and spend Eid morning ensuring that thousands of families can celebrate with a warm, nourishing meal.

وَأَطْعِمُوا الْقَانِعَ وَالْمُعْتَرَّ "Feed the one who asks and the one who does not ask." — The Qur'an teaches us to feed with open hands, not waiting for people to beg. — Surah Al-Hajj 22:36

This Eid ul Fitr 2026, we need your help more than ever. The cost of food has risen, but the need in our communities has risen further. 40 pots. R140,000. 5,000 people. This is the goal — and it is achievable with your support.

Our Journey

13 Years of Service

2012 — The Beginning
Ash Shakoor Benevolent Trust cooks its first pots of food for the community in Hanover Park, driven by a simple belief: no one should go hungry on Eid.
2014–2016 — Growing Reach
The feeding programme expands to Manenberg and Kensington as word spreads and more community volunteers join the cause.
2018–2020 — Tafelsig & Gouda
Operations extend to Tafelsig and Gouda, reaching communities outside Cape Town. Despite challenges of COVID-19, we find ways to continue feeding.
2022–2024 — Scaling Up
With growing support from local donors, the number of pots increases year on year. The trust establishes itself as a trusted pillar of community welfare across five areas.
2026 — Our Biggest Campaign Yet
For the first time, we are launching a digital fundraising campaign to reach our most ambitious goal: 40 pots, 5,000 people, one Eid morning. Donate directly via EFT to help us get there.
